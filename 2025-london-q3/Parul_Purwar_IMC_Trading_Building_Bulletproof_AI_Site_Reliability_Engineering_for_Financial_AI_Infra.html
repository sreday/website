Building Bulletproof AI: Site Reliability Engineering for Financial AI Infrastructure at Scale

Parul Purwar
IMC Trading

As the financial AI market grows rapidly toward a projected $78.6 billion by 2030, Site Reliability Engineers face unprecedented challenges in maintaining high availability for mission-critical AI systems processing millions of transactions daily. This presentation reveals how SRE practices are evolving to support AI-driven financial services where milliseconds matter and downtime is costly. Drawing from real-world implementations across trading firms, banks, and fintech companies, we'll explore the unique reliability challenges of AI systems that handle customer service inquiries, process fraud detection, and manage credit assessments analyzing hundreds of variables in real-time. Unlike traditional applications, these AI workloads exhibit unpredictable resource consumption patterns, complex dependency chains, and failure modes that can cascade across financial ecosystems. Key focus areas include architecting resilient ML pipelines that maintain fast response times under peak loads, implementing chaos engineering practices designed for AI model degradation scenarios, developing observability strategies for AI systems where traditional monitoring approaches fall short, and creating automated failover mechanisms that preserve model accuracy while ensuring business continuity. We'll examine infrastructure strategies that enable payment systems to achieve significantly faster processing speeds while maintaining reliability, and explore how cloud-native SRE practices support the scaling of insurance risk models and complex financial AI workloads without performance degradation. This session will equip SRE professionals with practical frameworks for building, monitoring, and maintaining AI infrastructure that meets the demanding reliability requirements of financial services while scaling efficiently as AI adoption accelerates. Attendees will gain actionable strategies for implementing SRE principles in AI-driven financial organizations.
