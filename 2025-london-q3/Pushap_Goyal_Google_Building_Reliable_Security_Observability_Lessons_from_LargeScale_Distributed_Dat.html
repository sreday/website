Building Reliable Security Observability: Lessons from Large-Scale Distributed Database Operations

Pushap Goyal
Google

Modern site reliability engineering faces increasing complexity when operating security-sensitive distributed systems at scale. As organizations manage petabytes of security telemetry data across globally distributed infrastructure, traditional monitoring approaches often fall short in providing the reliability and observability needed for effective security operations. This presentation examines operational challenges and solutions in running large-scale distributed database systems for security observability. Drawing from real-world experience with globally distributed architectures, we'll explore how distributed consistency models, time synchronization challenges, and relationship-based data patterns impact system reliability. Key topics include managing distributed system complexity through proper data modeling, implementing effective monitoring and alerting for graph-based workloads, and maintaining system availability while processing high-velocity security event streams. We'll discuss practical approaches to handling time-series data consistency across distributed nodes and operational strategies for maintaining query performance at scale. The session covers reliability engineering practices specific to security data pipelines, including circuit breaker patterns for distributed graph queries, capacity planning for relationship-heavy workloads, and incident response strategies when security observability systems fail. Attendees will learn practical SRE approaches for operating complex distributed systems, real-world lessons about scaling graph-based architectures, and operational patterns that maintain system reliability while supporting security teams' analytical needs. Technical demonstrations will show monitoring and observability practices that have proven effective in production environments.

Pushap Goyal is a Senior Software Engineer with over 11 years of experience in the internet industry, specializing in designing and optimizing data-intensive applications for massive scale. He has built a career at Google, Meta, and Amazon, focusing on distributed systems, databases, and high-performance computing using C++ and Linux. At Google since November 2022, Pushap serves as a Senior Software Engineer on the Spanner team, working on Google's globally distributed database that powers mission-critical applications including Maps, Search, Gmail, and YouTube. His work impacts systems handling 4 billion queries per second and managing 15 exabytes of data with five 9s availability. He has led a multi-year initiative that achieved a 50% cost reduction and 2x throughput improvement on the same hardware, enhancing Spanner's price-performance ratio. He has also spearheaded the development of Digital Markets Act (DMA) compliance solutions within Spanner, focusing on Wave 2 to enforce product-level data compliance. His contributions earned him the Google Cloud Tech Impact Award for his work on Spanner Price Performance. During his tenure at Meta (December 2019 - November 2022), Pushap played a key role in driving the internal adoption of MyRocks (MySQL + RocksDB) as a high-performance storage engine across Meta's social graph, including Facebook and Instagram. His work generated millions in operational cost savings by making MyRocks the default storage engine. He led the multi-year migration from MySQL 5.6 to 8.0, successfully porting over 1,700 custom code patches and adapting to API changes across Meta's infrastructure. His work on database availability, utilizing Kudu Raft-based replication (MyRaft), reduced worst-case service downtime from 180 seconds to 6 seconds. He contributed 56 open-source commits to Facebook's MySQL repository and authored the research paper "MyRaft: High Availability in MySQL using Raft" published in EDBT 2024. Pushap's time at Amazon (June 2014 - December 2019) included work on Amazon Timestream, where he architected the query infrastructure for the serverless time-series database, leading a team of five engineers to deliver scalable solutions for analytical queries of varying sizes. As a core member of the Amazon DocumentDB launch team, he contributed to building a MongoDB-compatible database service that reached over 5,000 customers and generated over $200M in revenue. His work included designing and implementing indexing solutions, including geo-spatial indexes, and developing infrastructure for customer visibility into operations and resource management. His innovations earned him patent US12093276B1 for "Emulating a non-relational database on a relational database with a distributed data store." Pushap's technical expertise spans databases including Spanner, MySQL, PostgreSQL, RocksDB, DocumentDB, Amazon Timestream, and KuduRaft, with programming skills in C++ and Python on Linux systems. His cloud platform experience covers AWS and Google Cloud, with deep knowledge in distributed systems, data structures, algorithms, scalability, high availability, performance optimization, and system design. He holds a B.Tech in Computer Science from Delhi Technological University (2010-2014).
