SRE in Gaming Tech: Handling Millions of Real-Time Requests

Siddharth Vijay
Baazi Games

In the gaming industry, every millisecond matters. When managing a real-time, high-traffic platform like PokerBaazi (India's biggest online poker platform), where millions of users are simultaneously playing, the need for Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) becomes critical. Achieving seamless performance, scalability, and reliability under heavy loads requires a strong blend of infrastructure, processes, and a dedicated SRE mindset.

In this session, we’ll explore how SRE practices play a key role in ensuring consistent uptime and performance for gaming platforms with massive concurrent user traffic. I will take you through:

  • The unique challenges of maintaining a highly available platform that handles millions of real-time requests daily.
  • The use of Kubernetes, microservices, and cloud-native architectures to manage scale while maintaining reliability.
  • How we leverage observability, alerting, and automated incident response to meet stringent SLAs in the gaming industry.
  • The role of Chaos Engineering in proactively testing platform stability and avoiding disruptions during peak times.
  • Scaling infrastructure seamlessly during high-demand gaming events (like online tournaments and special events) while ensuring low latency.

This session is ideal for SREs, DevOps engineers, and platform architects working in high-traffic environments, especially in the gaming and real-time tech space. You’ll walk away with practical strategies and insights on how to balance performance, scalability, and resilience while handling millions of requests in real time.
